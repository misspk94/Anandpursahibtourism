Why visit

A calm, sacred, and deeply historical town

Anandpur Sahib rewards slow travel. Begin with ardas at Takht Sri Keshgarh Sahib, walk through the old qila sites, then spend the afternoon inside Virasat-e-Khalsa's galleries. During Hola Mohalla, the town becomes a powerful celebration of devotion, martial tradition, music, and sangat.

Places to see

Highlights of Anandpur Sahib

Sacred Site

Takht Sri Keshgarh Sahib

One of Sikhism's five takhts and the revered site associated with the founding of the Khalsa by Guru Gobind Singh Ji in 1699.

Museum

Virasat-e-Khalsa

A dramatic museum complex that tells stories of Sikh heritage, Punjabi culture, and the formation of the Khalsa.

Qila Trail

Historic Qilas

Follow the memory of Anandgarh, Lohgarh, Holgarh, Fatehgarh, and Taragarh, linked with Guru Gobind Singh Ji's time in the city.

Travel essentials

Plan respectfully and comfortably

At Gurdwaras

Cover your head, remove shoes, dress modestly, and keep photography rules in mind.

Getting There

Arrive by road from Chandigarh or Rupnagar, or use Anandpur Sahib railway station.

Food

Enjoy langar with sangat, then try Punjabi meals in the town market after sightseeing.

For Families

Start early, keep museum time unhurried, and carry water in warmer months.
